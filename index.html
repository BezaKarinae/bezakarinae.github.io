Tatra Mountains

Welcome to the Tatra Mountains, a natural treasure of Central Europe. Explore the rich flora and fascinating fauna that make this region unique.

About Tatras

The Tatra Mountains, forming the natural border between Poland and Slovakia, are the highest range of the Carpathians. This region is a sanctuary for biodiversity, hosting thousands of plant species and numerous animal species. From alpine meadows and rocky peaks to dense forests and crystal-clear streams, the Tatras offer a diverse landscape that supports both common and rare forms of life. The mountains are protected by national parks, ensuring the survival of endangered species and the preservation of pristine habitats. Whether you are a nature enthusiast, a hiker, or a photographer, the Tatras provide endless opportunities to connect with the natural world.

Flora

The Tatras are home to over 1,500 plant species, including rare alpine flowers like edelweiss and gentian. Explore colorful meadows, dense conifer forests, and unique endemic plants found only in this region
